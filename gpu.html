GPU- Graphical Processing Unit

How does a GPU works?

A GPU, or Graphical Processing Unit, is a specialized electronic circuit designed primarily to rapidly manipulate and alter memory to accelarate the creation of images in aframe buffer intended for output to a display devcie.

NVIDIA GPU.

NVIDIA GPUs, produced by NVIDIA Corporation, are among the most popular and powerful graphics processing units available. NVIDIA GPUs receive driver updates and support from NVIDIA for an extended period after their initial release.

AMD GPU.

AMD GPUs, produced by Advanced Micro Devices, are another prominent line of graphics processing units known for their performance and features. AMD's latest GPU architecture, RDNA (Radeon DNA), is designed to deliever high performances, power effeciency, and features for gaming and professional applications.

GPU for gaming.

A GPU for gaming is a specialized electronic component designed specifically to render and process graphics in video games. It is responsible for rendering images, textures, and visual effects in real-time, creating the immersive gaming experiences that players enjoy.
